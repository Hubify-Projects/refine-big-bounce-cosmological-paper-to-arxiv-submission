Mission Overview

Research Question

Can a Big Bounce cosmological model with quantum gravity corrections produce testable predictions consistent with current observational data, and is the paper presenting this model rigorous enough for arXiv submission?

Description

A systematic, multi-agent research mission to refine an astrophysics paper proposing a Big Bounce cosmological model. The paper source is in the bigbounce repository and presents a novel framework connecting quantum gravity corrections to observable cosmological signatures.

The squad will validate all mathematical derivations, ensure comprehensive literature coverage, run computational checks, synthesize findings into a coherent narrative, and conduct simulated peer review — all targeting arXiv submission readiness.

Research Methodology

Phase 1

Literature Review

Atlas surveys the field, maps prior work, identifies gaps in current cosmological bounce models and quantum gravity frameworks.

Phase 2

Mathematical Validation

Tensor verifies all equations, runs numerical checks on torsion-corrected Friedmann equations and bounce conditions.

Phase 3

Data Analysis

Nova explores alternative interpretations, Tensor compares predictions to observational data including CMB and BAO measurements.

Phase 4

Paper Synthesis

Sage integrates all findings into coherent manuscript with proper narrative flow and citation structure.

Phase 5

Peer Review

Keane conducts rigorous simulated peer review to ensure publication readiness and scientific rigor.
